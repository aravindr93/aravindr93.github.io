Short Bio

I am an undergraduate student at IIT Madras, affiliated with the RISE lab and ILDS initiative. I have had wonderful experiences working with Profs. Ravindran, Shankar Narasimhan, and Sridharaumar. For my work on topological graph search in context of computational sustainability, I recieved the best undergraduate thesis award from IIT Madras. Previously, I was a summer intern at IMSc CS and statistical physics with Sitabhra Sinha. From Autumn 2016, I'll be a PhD student at University of Washington with Profs. Sham Kakade and Sergey Levine. I also hope to collaborate with other researchers across EECS, statistics, and math.

My research interests are broad and include optimization, machine learning, network science, and control. Some questions I have been thinking about are:

  • Theory and algorithms for interactive learning (RL, bandits, active etc.)

  • Deep reinforcement learning and computer vision for robotics

  • Common representation learning for multi-task, multi-view, and multi-modal settings

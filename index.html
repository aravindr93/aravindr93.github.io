Short Bio

I am an undergraduate student at IIT Madras, affiliated with the RISE lab and ILDS initiative. I have had wonderful experiences working with Profs. Balaraman Ravindran and Shankar Narasimhan on machine learning and optimization. For my work on topological graph search in context of computational sustainability, I received the best undergraduate thesis award from IIT Madras. Before I shifted my focus to AI, I worked in statistical physics with Dr. Sitabhra Sinha. From Autumn 2016, I'll be a PhD student at University of Washington with Profs. Sham Kakade and Sergey Levine.

My research interests are broad and include optimization, machine learning, control, and complex networks. Some questions I have been thinking about are:

  • Theory and algorithms for interactive learning (RL, bandits, active etc.)

  • Deep reinforcement learning and computer vision for robotics

  • Common representation learning for multi-task, multi-view, and multi-modal settings
